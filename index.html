About Me

Hello Friend, I am a 25 year old unsigned producer. I currently work full-time as a software engineer, and produce in my free time with the aspiration to create stage worthy music and beyond.

Growing up I always loved computers and had wanted to build them. Somewhere down the road around 14 or 15 I was gifted Fruity Loops and begun casually writing music and software for fun. I loved both hobbies, and ended up perusing a degree in Computer engineering and Computer Science at the University of Massachusetts.

While there I took graduate level computer science, machine learning, signal processing, ect.. courses. This was a very hard time in my life, but I managed to live a social lifestyle and still succeed. One constant throughout all the mayhem of engineering were my close friends. One such friend was producing music for a record label at the time, which inspired me to keep up with music production as a hobby, to start learning new tools, and how to better myself with my production techniques.

Every so often I would go over to his music studio and we would have “studio sessions” where we would try to work together. They almost always ended in failure to complete a production, but I did learn many new things. This included the ability to DJ, put a production together in a readable way, advanced sound design techniques, and much more.

At one point I was in an Artificial Intelligence course, and was doing homework when I saw a music video of a 3D hologram of 2-Pac on YouTube. It dawned on me that one day I could maybe automate a piece of software to play music on a stage like they did in that video. Taking this a step further I thought about creating AI to write music. I ended up calling this idea/application “r3currsion”.

After graduating college I started working full-time as a developer, and on the side become very passionate about music. I will never be able to give up writing software, and the addiction to solving complex logical problems, as I will never be able to give up my creative side of making unique works of art in my digital studio. So far these two talents have worked in tandem allowing my brain to start to create new out-side of the box solutions at work, as well as learn audio production software faster.

Lets see where the future leads!
